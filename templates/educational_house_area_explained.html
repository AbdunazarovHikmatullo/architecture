The Precision of Gross Floor Area

Gross Floor Area (GFA) is the foundational metric in architectural planning. It encompasses the total floor area contained within the building envelope, measured to the external face of the external walls. Unlike simple residential "square footage," GFA calculation requires a rigorous engineering approach to vertical voids.

"In architectural precision, space is not just what you walk on; it is the volume that remains when the structure is subtracted."
— Lead Engineer, Architecton

Common misconceptions arise in the treatment of "voids" and "mezzanines." For instance, a double-height ceiling in a living room is counted as GFA only on the level where the floor plate exists. However, for structural load and thermal performance calculations, the total volume must be accounted for as "Disclosed Air Space."
